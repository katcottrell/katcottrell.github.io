SCRUM

SCRUM is a framework, also most often used in software development, for product development. SCRUM breaks production into iterations called “sprints,” in which small teams plan goals, attempt to complete them with frequent team check-ins, and discuss successes and challenges sprintly. SCRUM is a great way to keep a team on track and ensure that practices are responsive to the needs of the production process. I also used SCRUM in my internship at Nextech Systems and in software design courses at Duke.
